Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 19, 2026  ·  Last updated: March 19, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the Go News mobile application ("the app", "we", "our") handles information when you use the app on Android or iOS. Please read this policy carefully. By using the app you acknowledge you have read and understood it.

1. Developer Information

The app is developed and maintained by Mina Andrawos.
For privacy-related inquiries, please open an issue at the project's GitHub repository.

2. Summary: No Personal Data Is Collected

Go News does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal or sensitive user data. There are no user accounts, no registration, no login, no analytics, and no advertising.

3. Data the App Does Not Collect

The app does not collect any of the following:

  • Name, email address, phone number, or any personally identifiable information
  • Precise or approximate location
  • Financial or payment information
  • Photos, videos, or audio
  • Contacts, calendar, or SMS data
  • Device identifiers (Advertising ID, IMEI, etc.)
  • App usage statistics or crash reports
  • Health or fitness data
  • Browsing history

4. Network Requests

The app makes outbound HTTPS requests to retrieve public RSS feeds from golangnews.com. These requests contain no user-identifiable information — only the standard network headers sent by any HTTPS client (such as IP address, which is handled by your network provider, not the app).

All data is transmitted over encrypted connections (TLS/HTTPS). No data is stored on any server operated by this app.

5. Third-Party Websites (WebView)

When you tap an article, the linked website opens in an in-app browser (WebView). That website is operated by a third party and is governed by its own privacy policy. We have no control over, and take no responsibility for, the content or privacy practices of any third-party website.

The in-app browser has standard web storage enabled, which means third-party websites may store cookies or local data on your device as permitted by their own privacy policies. The app itself does not read, write, or access any of that data.

6. Third-Party SDKs and Services

The app does not integrate any of the following:

  • Analytics or tracking SDKs (e.g., Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude)
  • Advertising networks
  • Crash-reporting services (e.g., Sentry, Crashlytics)
  • Social login providers

The app is built with Expo / React Native. The Expo runtime included in the app does not independently collect or transmit personal data.

7. Device Permissions

PermissionPurposeData collected
INTERNET Fetch RSS feeds from golangnews.com over HTTPS None — no user data is sent

No other device permissions are requested or used.

8. Data Retention

Because no personal data is collected, there is no user data to retain or delete. The app does not store any information beyond the current device session's in-memory state (the list of articles currently displayed on screen).

9. Your Rights

Because no personal data is collected, there is no stored data to access, correct, export, or delete. If you believe any data about you has been processed in error, please contact us via the GitHub repository linked above.

10. Children's Privacy

The app is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us immediately.

11. Changes to This Policy

If our data practices change, this policy will be updated and the effective date at the top of this page will be revised. Significant changes will also be noted in the app's Play Store / App Store release notes. Continued use of the app after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
